Foundations & Endowments

The mission is permanent. The portfolio should act like it.

Foundations, endowments, and nonprofit boards hire us to do one thing well: keep the spending promise — this year, next decade, and after every board member at the table has rotated off. Independent, fee-only, and fiduciary to the institution itself.

Every basis point of cost and every point of drawdown is a grant that doesn't get made. We manage like that's true — because it is.

What Boards Hire Us For

Stewardship you can defend in the minutes.

Spending policy that survives bad decades
We model your draw — 4%, 5%, or a smoothing rule — against real sequences of returns, not averages, and design the portfolio backward from the distribution schedule. The goal is intergenerational equity: today's grants funded without quietly spending tomorrow's corpus.
Investment policy statements that actually govern
We draft or refresh the IPS with your board — objectives, spending rule, asset-allocation ranges, rebalancing discipline, and restriction language — so every future decision has a written standard, and every auditor's question has an answer.
Board-ready reporting
Quarterly reporting through institutional-grade Black Diamond: performance against policy benchmarks, spending capacity, and attribution in plain English. Your investment committee spends its meeting on decisions, not on decoding statements.
UPMIFA-aware fiduciary process
Documented diligence, minutes-ready rationale, and prudence standards aligned with UPMIFA and your state's requirements. As a fee-only adviser we sell no products and accept no commissions — the only economics in the room are yours.
Mission-aligned when you want it
Screens, ESG tilts, or program-related carve-outs implemented with discipline — and with honest analysis of what each constraint costs, so the board chooses with eyes open.
Standards
Firm-wide GIPS® compliance since inception. Composite performance, Form ADV, and due-diligence materials provided to qualified institutions on request. Principals are CFA® Charterholders, University of Chicago Booth-trained. Women- and minority-owned — we welcome inquiries from diverse-manager programs.
