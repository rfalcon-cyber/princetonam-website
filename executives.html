Executives

Your pay package is complex. Your plan shouldn't be.

Equity that vests on the company's schedule, not yours. Blackout windows that close right when you need to act. A tax return with more moving parts than most small businesses. We coordinate all of it — with your counsel and CPA — so decisions get made on purpose, not by default.

Where Executives Get Stuck

Five pain points we see in nearly every engagement.

Concentration risk from RSUs and options
Years of grants pile up into a single-stock position that can dwarf everything else you own. Selling on your own schedule is rarely simple — trading windows, 10b5-1 plans, and insider-reporting rules all shape when and how it can happen. Read the four exits besides selling →
Blackout windows and 10b5-1 timing
The stock often moves the most exactly when you're locked out of trading it. A 10b5-1 plan put in place ahead of time — and reviewed with your legal team — turns a scramble into a schedule.
Deferred comp and NQDC elections
Nonqualified deferred compensation isn't protected the way a 401(k) is — it's an unsecured promise from the company, and distribution elections lock you in for years. We model the payout against your tax bracket, retirement date, and the company's own credit risk.
ISO exercises and the AMT trap
Exercising incentive stock options can trigger the alternative minimum tax in a year you never see coming, on paper gains you haven't actually realized. Exercise timing has to be planned against your full tax picture, not decided in isolation.
Life after the corner office
Board seats, advisory work, consulting, or full retirement — the income that replaces a W-2 rarely looks like the W-2 did. We build the transition plan well before the exit date, not after.
